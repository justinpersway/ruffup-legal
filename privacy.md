# ruffup Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026-05-15_

This is the privacy policy for ruffup, an iOS alarm app. It explains what data the app collects, where it goes, and what choices you have.

**The short version:** Onboarding answers, alarms, streaks, and mission results stay on your device. The only data that leaves your phone is anonymous subscription state that the app needs to confirm you're a paying subscriber.

## 1. What stays on your device

The following is stored only in the local SwiftData store on your iPhone and is never transmitted to ruffup or any third party by us:

- Alarm schedules (time, weekdays, mission, sound)
- Onboarding answers (wake time, difficulty rating, pain points, mission preferences, goal)
- Streak history and mission results
- Camera images captured during fetch missions (used in-memory only — the image is analyzed by Apple's on-device Vision framework and then discarded; nothing is uploaded)
- App preferences (theme, disabled missions, etc.)

If you delete the app, this data is removed with it.

## 2. Subscription data (RevenueCat + Apple)

To gate paid features we use **RevenueCat** (https://www.revenuecat.com/), which wraps Apple's StoreKit framework. When you open the paywall or complete a purchase, the following is processed:

- An **anonymous, random user identifier** generated by the RevenueCat SDK. It is not tied to your name, email, Apple ID, or any account.
- **Subscription status** — which subscription package you bought, when it was purchased, when it renews or expires, and whether your trial converted.
- **Device + locale info** Apple sends with every StoreKit transaction (App Store country, iOS version, app version).

This is sent to RevenueCat's servers so the app can confirm your entitlement on subsequent launches without you needing to sign in. RevenueCat's privacy policy is at https://www.revenuecat.com/privacy.

We do not associate this data with any other personal identifier, and we do not sell or share it for advertising.

## 3. Notification permission

If you allow notifications, the app uses **AlarmKit** (iOS 26+) or **UserNotifications** (older iOS) to schedule local alarm notifications on your device. These never leave your phone. If you decline, the app still works but your alarms will not fire while it's in the background.

## 4. Permissions

ruffup may ask for:

- **Notifications** — required for alarms to fire.
- **Camera** — required if you choose a camera-based wake mission (e.g., "fetch the sink"). Used in-memory only.

You can revoke either at any time in iOS Settings → ruffup.

## 5. Children

ruffup is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from them.

## 6. Your rights

Because nothing personal leaves your device (except the anonymous subscription identifier above), there is no account to delete on our side. Removing the app removes the on-device data. To stop being identified by RevenueCat, you can also reset your device's advertising ID in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking, although this does not affect entitlement validation.

## 7. Changes

If we materially change how data is handled, we'll update this page and the "Last updated" date above.
